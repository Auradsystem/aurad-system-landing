## Our Ranking Methodology

To keep our rankings objective, we scored each platform (out of 5.0) against three weighted categories, which reflect the primary AI use cases identified in [Loopio’s RFP Trends Report.](https://loopio.com/trends-report/)

### 1\. Generative Precision (40%)

- **Draft accuracy:** The ability of the AI to move from a blank document to a 90% first draft using internal knowledge sources.
- **Contextual editing:** How well the tool performs verbiage revisions, such as changing passive voice to active or simplifying technical jargon for specific audiences.
- **Source attribution:** The presence of verified citations that link every AI-generated response back to its original source document for content trust.

### 2\. Winning Insights (35%)

- **Market & competitive insight:** The tool’s capability to analyze historical bid data and market trends to identify competitive advantages and win probability.
- **Win theme mapping:** How effectively the AI recommends messaging that resonates with the client by comparing internal strengths against specific pain points.
- **Opportunity assessment:** The use of predictive analytics to support “Go/No-Go” decisions by identifying high-probability matches based on successful submissions.

### 3\. Agentic Workflow (25%)

- **Document analysis:** The speed and accuracy with which the AI “shreds” a new RFP to auto-detect questions and parse requirements from complex formats.
- **Operational orchestration:** Features that reduce manual work by automating logistical tasks like formatting and follow-ups with subject matter experts (SMEs).
- **Process transparency:** The robustness of audit trails that track every AI-generated edit, ensuring compliance and accountability throughout the response lifecycle.

## Can You Use OpenAI Tools to Respond to RFPs?

We know many proposal teams will try OpenAI tools, like ChatGPT or Claude, first. Both are fast, accessible, and often the easiest way to experiment with AI for drafting answers, summarizing requirements, or rewriting content under tight deadlines.

However, these tools operate outside of dedicated RFP workflows. They don’t have native awareness of approved content libraries, response governance, version control, or collaboration requirements. Accuracy depends heavily on prompting and manual review, and teams must manage data handling and compliance considerations carefully.

As a result, these AI tools are best used as a supplementary drafting tool alongside dedicated RFP software, rather than as a primary system for managing responses.
